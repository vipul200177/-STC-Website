Patient Dashboard Challenge

April 10th-12th

Details

Problem Statement

The teams are supposed to develop a patient timeline dashboard which will contain all the information about a patient’s medical history. The dashboard should be in the form of an application that can be accessed by doctors as well as the patients.

  • The doctors can add details like diagnosis, prescription, treatment, etc to a particular patient’s dashboard with timestamps.
  • The patients can view those details via a portal in the form of a web or mobile application.
  • Any registered doctor can add new items to the dashboard and edit details added by him, but the history of changes made must be saved.
  • Doctors can also comment on previous entries.
  • Patients should be able to find nearby doctors and hospitals by the nature of the treatment required.
  • Hospital owners can also register their hospitals and manage all the doctors they have (Hospital needs to be government certified).
  • SOS service inside the patients’ app that will notify nearby ambulances.
  • More ideas and innovation are welcomed.


Judging Criteria

The evaluation will be on the basis of:

  1. Demonstration
  2. Presentation
  3. Mid-evaluation report


Other Details

  • This is a high-prep problem statement
  • A team can contain a maximum of 9 members
  • This problem statement will carry 200 points
  • All the features of the dashboard should be in working condition before the demonstration.
  • The mid-evaluation report needs to be submitted by each house by 1st April which will contain details about the idea, features, technologies, and frameworks that are going to be used to solve the problem.
  • The mid-evaluation report carries 50 points.
  • Each team will have a demonstration of a maximum of 10 minutes. The presentation must also be under 10 minutes followed by a QnA session of 5 minutes.
